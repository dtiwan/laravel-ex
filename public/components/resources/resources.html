Who should protest? And Why

Everyone! It is our collective responsibility to get our properties appraised at fair values. Keeping the appraised values down helps the neighbors which in turn helps values to stay reasonable in the future.

Is the deadline over?

For both Williamson and Travis county, the deadline to initiate protest (file 'Notice of Protest') is May 31, 2017.

What is the protest process?

Step 1: Appeal online

First, start with an online protest. In a few cases this has saved time and has led to success. However, most likely your appeal will be denied. But DO NOT accept that results.

Step 2: Schedule hearing

Act fast to schedule your informal hearing. The meeting only takes 10-15 minutes and can lead to better results. Slots get filled up pretty past. So act fast & reserve a slot that is as late as possible. This way you can also carry along evidence on other properties that have successfully protested.

Step 3: Go to hearing

This is your next chance to present your case with a county tax officer with as much research & evidence as possible. Your goal should be to gather enough evidence that your poperty is appraised higher than comparables and place the burden on the officer to prove otherwise.

Step 4: Go for binding arbitration

This step will likely cost you money and you are only eligible if you have homestead exemptions on your property. we advice you hire an attorney to help you case at this point.

Top 5 tips for a successful protest?

Based on advice from experts, the following are top considerations you ought to make in order to build a strong case of appeal.

1. Request the evidence packet county appraiser used to evaluate your property. This is your main source of ammunition for getting your value reduced.

2. Locate any disrepair on your property or any "Negative Influence" such as busy streets, nearby high tension electric wire, commercial property bordering your residential etc. and use them to strengthen your argument.

3. Take pictures (very important) as they are worth a thousand words and use them for building your case.

4. Recent sales and appraisal data on similar properties will further help your case.

5. Hire an agent if you are not confident or have time/resources to protest. It's worth it.

Should I hire an agent or do it myself?

It totally depends on your case and you. If you really don't want to go through the process, then it is better off engaging an agent to work on your behalf. About 68% of homeowners in Travis county employed an agent. But remember, the agents could charge you about 40% of the tax savings!
